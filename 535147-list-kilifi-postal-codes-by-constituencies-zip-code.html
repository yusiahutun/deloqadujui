Tourism in Kilifi County

Fishing and tourism are the major economic activities in Kilifi County due to its proximity to the Indian Ocean. The major tourist attractions in the county are the Malindi Museum, Ruins of Gedi, Watamu Marine National Park, Kaya Forest and Matungu Beach. It also has the Mnarani ruins that date between the 14th and 17th centuries.

Transport and communication in Kilifi County

Kilifi County boasts an extensive road network spanning 3,678.6 kilometres, with different surfacing types. Marram surfaces account for 1,091.5 kilometres, earth surfaces cover 2,230.9 kilometres, and bitumen surfaces extend over 356.2 kilometres. The primary highways in the county include Kaloleni Mavueni and Mombasa Malindi.

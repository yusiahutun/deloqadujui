9. Nestor Araujo

Néstor Araujo is a Mexican professional soccer player who plays as a center-back for Liga MX club América and the Mexico national team.

He began his career at Cruz Azul before joining Santos Laguna, where he played over 100 matches and won the Liga MX twice.

Araujo has represented Mexico in various international tournaments, including the 2011 Copa America, 2012 Toulon Tournament, Copa America Centenario, and the 2017 FIFA Confederations Cup.

He was also part of the Mexico team that won the gold medal at the 2012 Summer Olympics.

8. Erick Aguirre

The 26 Year-old Érick Germáin Aguirre Tafolla (born 23 February 1997) is a talented Mexican professional soccer player who currently serves as a left-back for Liga MX club Monterrey.

Aguirre made his senior debut with Morelia on 8 August 2014 in a goalless draw. He scored his first goal for the team on 19 September 2014, although they ultimately lost 3-2 against Pumas UNAM.

On 8 June 2016, Aguirre was acquired by Pachuca, where he continued to showcase his skills. He scored his debut goal for the club on 20 January 2018 in a 3-1 victory against Lobos BUAP.

In a significant move, on 3 July 2021, Aguirre joined Monterrey, further cementing his presence in Mexican football.

7. Carlos Salcedo

Carlos Joel Salcedo Hernández (born 29 September 1993), known as El Titán, is a Mexican professional soccer player who currently plays as a center-back for Liga MX club Cruz Azul.

He began his youth career at Guadalajara before moving on to Tigres UANL and Real Salt Lake, where he made his professional debut in 2013.

Salcedo later returned to Guadalajara and had loan spells at Fiorentina and Eintracht Frankfurt before permanently joining the latter in 2018.

He has been a member of the Mexican national team since 2015, representing his country in major tournaments such as the Copa América, CONCACAF Gold Cup, Olympics, Confederations Cup, and World Cup.

6. Edson Alvarez

Edson Omar Álvarez Velázquez is a highly skilled Mexican professional soccer player who currently plays as a defensive midfielder or center-back for Eredivisie club Ajax and the Mexico national team.

Álvarez received his first call-up to the senior national team in January 2017 for a friendly match against Iceland.

He made his senior debut on 8 February of the same year, coming on as a substitute in the 60th minute.

Álvarez was also part of the supporting practice squad for the Confederations Cup team, along with Alejandro Mayorga.

In October 2022, Álvarez was named in Mexico’s preliminary 31-man squad for the World Cup.

Subsequently, in November, he was included in the final 26-man roster for the tournament.

Álvarez’s versatile skills, combined with his ability to play in different positions, have made him a valuable asset for both his club and the Mexican national team.

5. Diego Lainez

Diego Lainez Leyva (born 9 June 2000) is a talented Mexican professional footballer who currently plays as a winger for Liga MX club Tigres UANL, on loan from Real Betis, and represents the Mexico national team.

Lainez received his first senior national team call-up in August 2018, making his debut in a match against Uruguay.

In October 2020, he scored his first international goal against Algeria. Notably, Lainez scored a crucial goal in the Concacaf Nations League Final in June 2021.

Although he was named in Mexico’s preliminary World Cup squad in October 2022, he did not make the final roster.

4. Hector Herrera

Héctor Miguel Herrera López (born 19 April 1990) is a Mexican midfielder who currently plays for Major League Soccer club Houston Dynamo and the Mexico national team.

Herrera started his professional career at Pachuca before joining FC Porto, where he became an important player and served as club captain from the 2015-16 season.

He made 245 appearances for Porto, winning a Primeira Liga title and two Supertaça cups.

Herrera has represented Mexico in various international tournaments, including the Olympic Games, CONCACAF Gold Cup, FIFA World Cup, FIFA Confederations Cup, and Copa América Centenario.

3. Jesus Corona

The 30 year-old Jesus Corona, commonly known as Tecatito, is a Mexican winger who currently plays for La Liga club Sevilla and the Mexico national team.

He started his career with Monterrey before moving to FC Twente in the Netherlands.

In 2015, Tecatito joined Porto, where he achieved great success, winning three Primeira Liga titles. He has made 287 appearances for Porto.

On the international stage, Tecatito has represented Mexico since 2014, winning the 2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup and participating in the 2018 FIFA World Cup, the 2021 Gold Cup, and two Copa América tournaments.

2. Hirving Lozano

Hirving Rodrigo Lozano Bahena, commonly known as Chucky, is a Mexican winger who currently plays for Serie A club Napoli and the Mexico national team.

Lozano started his professional career with Pachuca, winning several titles, including the 2016 Clausura and the 2016-17 CONCACAF Champions League.

In 2017, he transferred to PSV Eindhoven in the Netherlands and won the Eredivisie in his first season, finishing as the team’s top scorer.

In 2019, Lozano became the most expensive Mexican player of all time when he signed for Napoli.

He has represented Mexico in various international tournaments, including the Copa América, FIFA Confederations Cup, and FIFA World Cup.

1. Raul Jimenez

Raúl Alonso Jiménez Rodríguez is a Mexican forward who plays for Wolverhampton Wanderers in the Premier League and the Mexico national team.

He began his career at Club América, winning the Clausura tournament in 2013. Jiménez then played for Atlético Madrid and Benfica, achieving success with the Portuguese side, including two league titles.

In 2018, he joined Wolves on loan and became their top-scorer, leading to a permanent transfer.

Jiménez suffered a serious head injury but made a successful return. He has represented Mexico in multiple tournaments, including FIFA World Cups, CONCACAF Gold Cup, Copa América, and the Olympics, and is among Mexico’s top goal-scorers.

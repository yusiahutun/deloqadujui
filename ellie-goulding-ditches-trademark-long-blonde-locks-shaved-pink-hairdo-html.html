Her long, feminine tresses became a trademark part of her style when she burst on the music scene in 2010.

But pop starlet Ellie Goulding boasted an altogether more edgy look as she revealed a pink new hairstyle.

In a stark departure from her usual style, the singer appeared to have shaved one side of her head with longer dyed pink strands pushed to one side and tied at the back.

Pink lady: Ellie Goulding at the launch of the Nobel Peace Prize concert in Oslo, Norway, sporting a dramatic new short hairstyle, which is shaved on one site of her head

Peace panel of pop: The singer (left) was joined by Amy Lee of the band Evanescence (middle) and Janelle Monae before the concert

The singer was one of the acts performing at the Nobel Peace Prize concert in Oslo, Norway. Ahead of the event she appeared at a press conference, joined on a panel by singers Amy Lee - from the band Evanescence - and Janelle Morae.

Goulding was clearly proud of being associated with the event, and tweeted: 'I performed at the Nobel Peace Prize ceremony in Oslo tonight with an orchestra. Words can't describe how proud I am. Off to celebrate! :-)'

She also posted: 'Did my first 10k this morning since I bust my knee... Happy to have you back knee! I've been rehearsing with so many amazing artists today.

'I'm so excitable! Running around doing capoeira and kicks and getting a lot of dodgy looks. Feeling high!!!!!'

British actress Dame Helen Mirren hosted the event with American star Rosario Dawson, which was held in honour of this year's winners -  Liberian President Ellen Johnson Sirleaf, women's rights activist Leymah Gbowee, from Liberia, and democracy activist Tawakkul Karman, from Yemen.

Dame Helen described it 'an extraordinary moment' when three women were awarded the prestigious Nobel Peace Prize.

Blonde bombshell: The singer's long blonde hair was a trademark part of her look

Good cause: The Nobel Peace Prize concert was hosted by Dame Helen Mirren (right) and American star Rosario Dawson

Speaking before the event, she said the award marks an 'extraordinary moment in the history of women' but it is 'slightly shameful statistically that only 12 women have won it in 112 years, when you think how important women historically have always been, specifically in terms of peace'.

She added: 'It is only a step on a journey that women are taking, and hopefully in 20-30 years' time we will be looking at a very different scenario in the world.

'In my personal experience, wherever there was a force for the positive, for creativity, it was almost always led by women and they are doing it with no recognition and under very difficult circumstances.

'It is so important for all of us to realise that these movements start in very, very small ways.'

Dame Helen said it was important for young women to have role models like this year's peace prize winners. She also said she has 'witnessed first hand' the struggle of women in Uganda.

Glamour: The concert was held in honour of the three women who won the Nobel Peace Prize this year

Hitting the right notes: Janelle Monae (left) performing during the concert, which was held at Oslo City Hall

She also pointed out that the three winners of the prize had come from small grass-root movements.

Rosario Dawson said she was inspired by the laureates during the ceremony Saturday in Oslo City Hall.

She added: 'I love being able to be part of this story and help these voices being as loud as possible because I believe in them and they are our future.'

The lineup of artists performing at the concert includes the Grammy Award-winning singer and activist from Benin, Angelique Kidjo, Yemen's Ahmed Fathi, Liberian-born singer Miatta Fahnbulleh, David Gray, Jill Scott and the World Youth Choir.

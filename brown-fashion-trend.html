Brown is set to rule this autumn, heres how to wear it

September 11, 2024
blog

Just as grey outfits (or ‘groutfits’ as TikTok predictably labelled them) have ruled for the past few months, another surprising neutral hue is set to take autumn by storm: brown. Seen all over the autumn/winter 2023 catwalks (everywhere from Prada to The Row), the colour – call it coffee bean or chocolate brown – is a great one to adopt into your wardrobe for its versatility and ease of styling.

We saw plenty of brown on the AW23 runways. At 16Arlington, not only was the catwalk floor covered in coffee grounds, but the colour dominated the collection. We saw brown leather coats and boots, as well as brown sequin mini dresses, proving that the hue does not have to be reserved for the daytime. At Brandon Maxwell, brown busty leather dresses and separates featured alongside cosy winter co-ords and a sleek dress in dark brown. Extremely covetable brown leather jackets hit the catwalk at Saint Laurent, while chocolate brown featured on pleated skirts, dresses and trousers at Fendi. We saw brown suede at Prada, Gucci fully emblazoned a skirt in its signature brown monogram, while Jacquemus gave some of its trademark slinky party dresses and corset tops a coffee-bean makeover.

Of course, the street-style set was quick to follow suit, while names such as Hailey Bieber, Kendall Jenner and Jessica Chastain have already made it work for the red carpet. But, what exactly is behind fashion’s new love affair with the colour brown?

“I think this stems from the ‘wardrobe necessities’ trend we’ve seen on the runways the past couple of seasons,” explains Libby Page, market director at Net-a-Porter. “We have seen an increase in customers’ interest towards elevated essentials with an element of wearability. It was refreshing to see relaxed, everyday clothes that could be paired with different pieces in our wardrobes throughout the year, and therefore with an increase in beige, white, black or brown looks.”

Neutral hues fit nicely into the concept of quiet luxury – they are understated, elegant and make for great investment pieces because they are easier to style than something bright and bold. “There is an understated luxury feeling with a lot of the more neutral tones,” agrees Liane Wiggins, head of womenswear at Matches Fashion. “I love a tonal look and a head-to-toe neutral.”

“The two are definitely linked,” Heather Gramston, senior head of buying at Browns, says of quiet luxury and the emergence of earthy browns on the catwalk. “As designers are using neutral colours, texture becomes super important too, which we saw with brown suede dominating bags and footwear – think tan suede accessories from Bottega Veneta and Celine’s knee-high ruched boot in chocolate brown suede.”

What also makes brown perhaps more appealing than other neutrals is not just that it’s easy to pair with other colours, but that it is super flattering, offering a much softer and less harsh alternative to black.

“Complementing all skin tones, brown is surprisingly easy to style and can be thought of as a neutral base to layer other colours and prints on, or wear it head to toe by pairing different textures alongside each other,” says Page.

Keen to give brown a go? We recommend investing in capsule classics with this – brown leather co-ords would make a great autumn investment (as demonstrated by Emily Ratajkowski last week) while brown tailoring would be a sleek update for your workwear wardrobe, and brown leather or suede accessories are a no brainer. And, don’t forget partywear.

“There are no hard and fast rules when it comes to styling brown pieces as they can transcend seasons and trends, but I love layering different shades of brown for an effortless chic look,” says Page on how best to wear the colour. “Accessorising with gold-tone watches and jewellery works well with all the shades of brown and would complement your look.”

With brown also dominating on the spring/summer 2024 catwalks (it was seen at Tom Ford, Ferragamo, Saint Laurent, Louis Vuitton and Loewe, to name a few), this neutral hue is set to be far more than a one-season wonder.

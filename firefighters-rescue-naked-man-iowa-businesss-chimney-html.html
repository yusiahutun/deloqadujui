Naked Iowa man Jordan Kajewski rescued by Carroll firefighters after being stuck in chimney

September 08, 2024
blog

A woman in Carroll, Iowa was surprised to discover a naked man had gotten stuck in the chimney of her recycling business.

Carrie Sapp, who co-owns the Carroll Redemption Center with her husband Brad, was working Wednesday morning when she heard a man say 'I'm in your chimney,' she told the local newspaper Daily Times Herald. 

'Don't call the cops,' the voice inside the chimney urged her. The mystery man went on to blame his predicament on a failed game of hide-and-seek. 

Jordan Kajewski, 29, was arrested for trespassing after he was discovered naked and stuck inside a chimney in Carroll, Iowa 

Lt. Tom Fransen, of the Carroll County, Iowa, Sheriff's office, shines a flashlight into the chimney where Kajewski was stuck

But she called the authorities anyway, and at 10:50am firefighters pounded a hole through the chimney and pulled the man out - which led to another surprise: the man was completely naked.

It was unclear why the man was naked. His clothes were also pulled out of the chimney, the Times Herald reported. 

'This was definitely a first for the Carroll Fire Department,' Carroll fire chief Greg Schreck told the Times Herald. 

The chimney is about a foot wide and 25 feet tall, the business owners said.

Police arrested the soot-covered man for trespassing, and identified him as Jordan Kajewski, 29, the Times Herald reported. 

Left, Carroll, Iowa, firefighters break apart a chimney to free a man who got stuck inside, apparently trying to break into Carroll Redemption Center. Right, the chimney seen from the outside

Brad Sapp told Daily Mail Online he believes he heard Kajewski make a noise the previous night, and that he might have been stuck in the chimney for up to 9 hours.

'On Tuesday night at about 2am, I was dragging bags out when all of a sudden I heard a whisper: "Get out of here!"'

'I didn't know if I was crazy or what,' Sapp said. 

When he told his wife about the whisper the next morning, she teased him for being 'afraid of ghosts,' he said. 

He said Kajewski had previously begged him for work, saying 'Just give me three hours!'

'Nobody wants a job for just three hours a week. My guess is he was trying to scope out the place,' Sapp said.

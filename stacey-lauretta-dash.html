Stacey Lauretta Dash- Professional Career

Dash made her acting debut in 1982, playing Denise Gray in “Farrell for the People,” and went on to feature in other supporting roles in shows such as “The Cosby Show” and “Moving.” She eventually joined the cast of “TV 101,” where she played Monique in 13 episodes from 1988 to 1989. She made a cameo in “The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air” in 1994 and worked on “Renaissance Man,” which allowed her the opportunity to work with actors such as Danny DeVito, Gregory Hines, James Remar, and Ed Begley Jr.

Dash was then cast as Dionne in “Clueless,” a critically praised comedy film about a rich and popular high school girl who tries to help a new student attain acceptance. She portrayed one of the film’s main characters, which helped her achieve recognition with fans. The following year, she joined the TV series based on the film and starred in all 62 episodes, eventually ending the project three years later. Dash worked on “Cold Around the Heart” and “Illegal in Blue” in the meanwhile.

After career

In the early 2000s, she worked on a variety of projects, including “CSI: Crime Scene Investigation,” a critically acclaimed crime television series, and “Paper Soldiers,” in which she played Tamika and collaborated with Kevin Hart, Jay Z, and her cousin Damon Dash. Stacey made an appearance in “View from the Top” in 2003, then the following year in Kanye West’s video for “All Falls Down.”

In 2007, she appeared as one of the main protagonists in “Ghost Image,” a thriller directed by Jack Snyder, and two years later, she joined the cast of “The Game” as Camille Rose. Stacy portrayed Valerie Stokes in “Single Ladies” in 2011, and she continued to work quickly in “The Thinning” as Kendra Birch. Her next projects include “Dawgtown,” “The Dawn,” and “The Seventh Secret.” Over the span of more than two decades, she has had close to 60 acting roles, and we are confident that there will be many more for this outstanding actress.

Santa Claus cast now tragic deaths, incurable illnesses and career U-turn

March 20, 2024
blog

Growing up there was no better way to get in the festive spirit than to sit around the TV in your pyjamas with your family, watching Christmas films including Muppets Christmas Carol, Home Alone and 1985's Santa Claus: The Movie.

Santa Claus: The Movie featured everything you could want from a Christmas film - music, laughter and a whole host of Hollywood A-listers bringing much loved characters to life and joy to generations of fans who still love the film.

Released on November 27, 1985 in time for the festive season, it brought joy to the box office too, making a huge $23.7million (£16.9million).

The Daily Star looks at where some of the cast of the much loved Christmas classic are now.

Dudley Moore - Patch

Lovable actor Dudley Moore played elf and inventor Patch in the 1985 Santa Claus film, showcasing his trademark humour in the role.

He was also a talented musician, winning a scholarship to study at Oxford before rising to fame as one half of comedy duo Peter Cook and Dudley Moore, and starring in hit comedies including 1981's Arthur.

He also starred in the sequel Arthur 2: On the Rocks, and performed onstage with his co-star Liza Minelli in concert in later years.

In 2001, he was diagnosed with progressive supranuclear palsy, which is an incurable brain condition similar to Parkinsons.

The Irish Times reported that Dudley talked about his illness in a television interview, saying: "There's always this feeling of why did it hit me?

"I cannot make peace with it because I know I am going to die from it. Yes I feel angry, that's true - to be reduced to this insignificant version of myself is overpowering."

In the same year, he was given a CBE by Prince Charles, attending the ceremony in a wheelchair as his health deteriorated due to the condition.

One year later, Dudley died aged 66 at his home in New Jersey on March 27, 2002.

David Huddleston - Santa Claus

Award winning actor David Huddleston played Santa Claus in the 1985 film, but before he was an actor, he had a career as a mechanic in the US Air Force for over four years.

He was already a huge star by the time he was in Santa Claus: The Movie, having appeared in films such as 1972's Bad Company, but one of the roles he will always be known for is Lebowski, in the Coen Brother's cult classic 1998 film The Big Lebowski.

In real life he was married twice, first to Carole Ann Swart from 1968, with whom he had a son Michael Huddleston, who is also an actor. However, Carole died in 1987.

He was then married to Sarah C Koeppe from 1999 to 2016.

David continued to act in films including 2005's The Producers and TV series, It's always sunny in Philadelphia, but died aged 85 on August 2, 2016.

John Lithgow - B.Z

John Lithgow played greedy toy maker B.Z in Santa Claus: The Movie, who tricks Patch into making a gift to help B.Z fall back into public favour.

Since then Lithgow hasn't left our screens, going on to play roles in huge series including Winston Churchill in The Crown, Dr Soloman in Third Rock from the Sun and voicing Lord Farquaad in comedy classic Shrek.

He has most recently appeared as Jud in the 2019 remake of Pet Cemetery, TV series Perry Mason and is due to appear in a number of films currently in post-production.

John has been married twice, first to Jean Taynton from 1966 to 1980, and then Mary Yeager from 1981.

He and Jean split as he had an affair with actress Liv Ullmann.

He has three children - Phoebe, Ian and Nathan Lithgow.

Judy Cornwell - Mrs Claus

The lovable Mrs Claus was played by Judy Cornwell in the film, but viewers might recognise her from another huge role in a popular British TV series – Keeping Up Appearances.

Judy played Daisy, the long suffering sister of Hyacinth Bucket from 1990 to 1995.

From there, she starred in programmes including EastEnders as Queenie from 2007 to 2008 and Birds of a Feather as Annie in 2014.

In 2015, she was rushed to hospital after collapsing before a performance of Agatha Christie's A Murder is Announced, at the Floral Pavillion Theatre in New Brighton, Wirral.

However she has since recovered and currently lives with her husband John Kelsall Parry, who she has been married to since 1960. They have one child, Edward Parry, together.

Carrie Kei Heim - Cornelia

Carrie Kei Heim played B.Z's niece in The Santa Claus, who becomes friends with an orphan called Joe, and together, they help save Christmas and Patch from B.Z.

Having been a child actress, her last role was in 1986's The Parent Trap 2.

Since then, Carrie has changed careers and has become a lawyer, after studying at the University of Pennsylvania School of Law.

In 2005, she also married husband Peter K. Binas, with who she has one child.

Christian Fitzpatrick - Joe

Christian Fitzpatrick played orphan Joe in Santa Claus: The Movie, who after helping save Christmas, goes to live in the North Pole with Santa Claus and Mrs Claus.

His next film role was in comedy film Vice Versa opposite Judge Reinhold in 1988, but since then, no further film roles have been listed for the actor.

Education

Speaking of his educational background, Higgins graduated from Walter Johnson High School in North Bethesda, Maryland after attending La Jolla High School. Later, he enrolled at Amherst College in Amherst to further his education.

Career and Professional Life

Talking about his career, Higgins made his Broadway debut in La Bête in 1991, and in 2000, he appeared in a production of Edward Albee’s Tiny Alice off-Broadway at Second Stage Theatre. In 1993’s Off-Broadway production of Paul Rudnick’s play Jeffrey, he also created the title role.

Further, Among Higgins’ many television appearances are roles on Boston Legal, Brother’s Keeper, Ally McBeal, and Honey, I Shrunk the Kids: The TV Show. He played the part of Kurt, Elaine’s boyfriend, in Seinfeld Season 8. Additionally, he appeared in five episodes of Arrested Development as lawyer Wayne Jarvis.

Higgins’ appearances in the movies Fun with Dick and Jane, Pitch Perfect and its follow-ups, and The Break-Up, for which he also contributed vocal arrangements, helped him gain more fame.

In addition to playing Judge Mentok in Harvey Birdman, 2401 Penitent Tangent in Halo 2, Attorney at Law, and the Riddler in Batman: The Brave and the Bold, he has also voiced other characters. In the HBO TV movie The Late Shift, Higgins also played David Letterman.

In David Hare’s Stuff Happens, which had its American premiere at the Mark Taper Forum in Los Angeles in 2004, he portrayed Secretary of Defense, Donald Rumsfeld. At the Lincoln Center Theater in New York, he performed the title role in A. R. Gurney’s Big Bill in 2003.

More about his career

2011 saw Higgins paired up with Fran Drescher in the movie Happily Divorced. He portrayed Peter Lovett, a character based on Drescher’s real-life ex-husband Peter Marc Jacobson, who was Fran’s gay ex-husband. Higgins was chosen to voice the conceited and bizarre businessman Iknik Blackstone Varrick in The Legend of Korra in 2012.

Higgins played Chuck Pierce on the NBC comedy series Great News from 2017 to 2018. Further, In 2018, Higgins took over as host of the game show America Says. In a season 3 episode of the TV comedy/drama detective series Monk 14 years earlier, he played a game show host.

In the Tracey Wigfield-created 2020 Peacock comedy series Saved by the Bell, Higgins played Principal Toddman.

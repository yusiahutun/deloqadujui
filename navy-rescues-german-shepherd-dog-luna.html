Navy Rescues German Shepherd Dog Luna

April 20, 2024
blog

On February 10, a one and a half year-old German Shepherd named Luna fell off a fishing boat and into the Pacific Ocean. Despite a frantic search, her owner thought all hope was lost. But five weeks later, the Navy found Luna alive and well, on an island 80 miles off the coast of San Diego, ABC News reports. 

[facebook ]https://www.facebook.com/NavalBaseCoronado/photos/... data-width="800"[/facebook]

"[Owner Nick Hayworth] told us Luna was a very powerful swimmer and that he was 90 percent sure she'd head for shore," Sandy DeMunnk, public affairs officer from Naval Base Coronado, told the network. "So our staff searched the island, but with no luck. He stayed in the area for two more days to look for her, and after a week, we considered her lost at sea and presumed dead."

[facebook ]https://www.facebook.com/NavalBaseCoronado/photos/pcb.10153920180083898/10153920165973898/?type=3&theater[/facebook]

But all of a sudden, Navy staff arriving on the island saw a cute little dog wagging her tail on the side of the road. They identified her as Luna and told Hayworth she was okay, and he was "jumping up and down" when he heard the news. A biologist for the San Diego Zoo examined her and said she likely survived by eating rats and dead fish while somehow surviving a tough swim and El Nino storms.

Fox News reports that the Navy turned over Luna to a friend of Hayworth's while he was out of town for work. But the two best buddies will shortly reunite in San Diego. In the meantime, the Navy has given Luna a replacement dog tag, that lists her name as well as a key lesson taught to all Navy personnel: "Keep the Faith."

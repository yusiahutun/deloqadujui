Los Angeles Clippers' Montrezl Harrell NBA Stats And Salary

Los Angeles Clippers' Montrezl Harrell won the 2019-20 Kia NBA Sixth Man Award for his successive year. Harrell, who finished third last year, registered the win for the first time this season. From a global panel of 100 sportswriters and broadcasters, Harrell earned 58 first-place votes and made 397 total points.

Behind him, Dennis Schröder from Oklahoma City Thunder came second with 35 first-place votes and 328 points in total. Similarly, Clippers guard Lou Williams, who is also a three-time Kia NBA Sixth Man Award winner, came third with the other seven first-place votes and 127 total points.

As per the NBA, players had the provision of five points for each first-place vote, three points for each second-place, and one point for each third-place vote. All the votes were given based on the match played through 11 March 2020. Players had to have come off the bench in more games than they start in games, which was one of the clauses of voting eligibility.

Montrezl Harrell's Stats And Salary

Montrezl Harrell, who stands at the height of 6 feet and 6 inches tall, averaged 18.6 points, 7.1 rebounds, and 1.14 blocked shots in 27.8 minutes in 63 games, where he started in two games through the games played through 11 March 2020. With it, he ranked second in the NBA in scoring (18.4 ppg) and fifth in rebounding (7.0 RPG).

Similarly, Harrell made an NBA-high 11 double-doubles, ranked second with 10 games of 25 or more points, as well as tied for first with four games of at least 30 points. Off the bench, he scored 34 points.

Moreover, Montrezl Harrell's career stats show that he played 318 games where he averaged 12.7 points per game, with 4.9 total rebounds, and 1.3 assists. Similarly, he recorded the field goal of 61.4 percent, a 3-points field goal of 10.0 percent, free-throw of 63.8 percent, with the win shares of 26.1.

Montrezl Harrell started his NBA career in 2015 from Houston Rockets. The Houston Rockets selected him in their 32nd overall pick in the 2015 NBA Draft, and in September 2015, he signed a three-year contract with the Rockets. He played a couple of seasons with the Rockets until 2017.

Later, he made his way to the Los Angeles Clippers in June 2017 and has been playing to this date. As per his deal with the Clippers, he makes an average salary of $6,000,000. He signed a two-year contract with the Los Angeles Clippers for $12,000,000 until the 2019/20 NBA Season.

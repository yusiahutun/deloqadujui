Eddie Murphy plays multiple roles

One of the standout features of “Coming to America” is Eddie Murphy’s incredible portrayal of not just one, but four different characters in the film. He plays the main character, Prince Akeem, as well as three other supporting characters, including Clarence the barber. This showcased Murphy’s versatility and comedic talent.

The movie was a box office success

Upon its release, “Coming to America” was a massive hit, grossing over $288 million worldwide. It became the highest-grossing comedy film of 1988 and cemented Eddie Murphy’s status as one of the biggest stars in Hollywood.

The film received critical acclaim

“Coming to America” was not only a financial success but also received positive reviews from critics. It was praised for its witty humor, memorable characters, and heartwarming storyline. The film currently holds a rating of 85% on Rotten Tomatoes.

The iconic McDowell’s restaurant

In the movie, Prince Akeem takes a job at McDowell’s, a fast-food restaurant that bears a striking resemblance to McDonald’s. The lure of McDowell’s and its hilarious fictional menu items, such as the “Big Mick,” became synonymous with the film.

The famous barbershop scenes

The barbershop scenes in “Coming to America” were some of the most memorable and comedic moments in the film. Eddie Murphy and Arsenio Hall played multiple characters in these scenes, engaging in hilarious banter and witty conversations.

The film features a star-studded cast

Aside from Eddie Murphy and Arsenio Hall, “Coming to America” boasts an impressive ensemble cast. It includes James Earl Jones, John Amos, Shari Headley, and a young Cuba Gooding Jr. This talented cast brought the story to life with their brilliant performances.

Akeem’s royal palace was a real mansion

The opulent royal palace where Prince Akeem resides in Zamunda is actually a mansion located in Bel-Air, California. The stunning estate served as the perfect backdrop for the fictional African kingdom.

Inspiration from Eddie Murphy’s life

Eddie Murphy drew inspiration from his own experiences for the storyline of “Coming to America.” He wanted to explore the concept of an African prince who ventures to America in search of true love, drawing on his own upbringing as the son of African immigrants.

The film features an iconic dance scene

One of the most memorable moments in the movie is the dance scene set to the hit song “The Bird” by The Time. Eddie Murphy and Arsenio Hall showcase their dancing skills, creating a fun and energetic sequence that has become iconic.

The film faced some controversy

“Coming to America” faced criticism from some African-American groups who believed it perpetuated stereotypes. However, Eddie Murphy defended the film, stating that it was intended to be a light-hearted comedy and not meant to offend.

Eddie Murphy’s makeup process

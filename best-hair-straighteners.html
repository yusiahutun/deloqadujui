What to Keep in Mind

Hair Type 

Since everyone’s hair is different, there’s no one universal hair straightener temperature that works best for all hair types and textures. In general, LA-based hair stylist Laurabeth Cabott says that to preserve the health of your hair, all hair types should try to keep their hot tools below 365 degrees,Although she adds that for anyone with color-treated hair, stick to an even lower temp. “Anything over 360 degrees can strip your hair color, she says.”

For anyone with fine hair, celebrity hairstylist and founder of Deeper Than Hair Annagjid Kee Taylor recommends using a low setting between 250-300 degrees. “Fine hair is delicate and can be damaged easily, especially by heat, so using a lower heat will help prevent breakage over time,” she explains.

If you have especially thick or heavy-bodied hair, celebrity hair artist Kieron Justin says you may need to bump it up to 450 degrees to see that pin-straight style. But Kee warns against going much higher, as “it will damage your strands and natural curl pattern.”

All of our experts agree before applying any heat you should always use a heat protectant first. And even better,“limit the use of the tool weekly, especially on colored hair,” adds Justin.

Plate Material 

Ceramic, titanium, and tourmaline are the three main plate types in hair straighteners. The most common is ceramic. “It distributes heat evenly, reducing ‘hot spots’ which leads to damage,” says Kee. Always double check that your flat irons are fully ceramic, says Justin. “Ifthey are not, they can snag hair, causing split ends and dry or damaged hair,” he says.

Meanwhile, titanium plates are strong, protective, and typically made from“lightweight metal. This material“heats up very quickly and maintains a consistent temperature,” says Kee. As titanium can withstand extremely high heat, she tells us they’re a great choice for those more skilled at using hot tools or someone with curly or coarse hair. 

If you plan on using your iron every day or have fine or thin hair, tourmaline plates might be the best option as they are the most gentle, thanks to their semi-precious stone coating. Tourmaline“emits negative ions once it heats up,” says Kee, which prevents static and frizz, while increasing shine.

Your Questions, Answered

How do you curl hair with a flat iron?

After applying a heat protectant and curl cream, Justin tells us to separate the hair into manageable sections, and Kee adds that it’s best to start with the bottom layers of hair first, going from the base of the head to the tip of the strand.

To create the curl, “run the irons from the root to the end, twisting the iron at 180 or 360 degrees slowly away from the face with the iron vertical until you reach the tips of the hair,” says Justin. Repeat this method around your head, changing the direction of how you curl the hair to add depth and dimension.

If you want a more natural look, Kee suggests allowing the curls to cool before combing through with your fingers and  giving your hair a good tousle. 

What type of flat iron is the least damaging?

“Ceramic is going to be your best bet if you are looking to straighten without compromising the integrity of your hair,” says Cabott. 

Kee agrees, adding that because these plates distribute heat evenly and use far-infrared heat to heat the hair from the inside out, “you’re reducing the amount of time you need to expose your hair to heat.”

Ceramic material also slides through hair seamlessly without pulling on strands. Just be sure to watch how often you’re ironing the same section of hair, says Kee, since these plates tend to heat up quickly and can disrupt your hair’s integrity when it lingers on hair or you’ve done one too many passes.

How do you clean a flat iron?

Cleaning your flat at least once a week keeps it in top-notch condition. Without a regular wipe down, build-up from heat protectants and other styling products can impact the amount of heat that is transferred from the plates to your hair, meaning that even with a high temperature, you’ll need to do more passes to get the same result. 

Before cleaning, ensure the iron is turned off and has completely cooled down. Then, Kee recommends using a damp cloth with a bit of rubbing alcohol and carefully wiping down the plates to remove any debris. She also likes to dip a cotton swab into rubbing alcohol to clean the iron’s edges and crevices.

“Don’t use any harsh or abrasive cleaning products as this can tarnish or damage the plates,” she says. For the most stubborn build-up, wipe the plates down with a damp, hot cloth, says Justin. “Just make sure it’s not drenched with water.”

Meanwhile Cabott opts for a microfiber cloth. “If you’re consistently seeing a lot of build-up, you may need to rethink your shampoo, conditioner or other products you are using.”

Once you’re done cleaning it, allow it to fully dry to avoid it rusting before storing it away.

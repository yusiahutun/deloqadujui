Jenna Ortega fans 'barely breathing' over her mini dress and cute poses

August 21, 2024
blog

Jenna Ortega has left fans stunned as she attended the Palm Springs International Film Festival, which is happening throughout January.

The Wednesday actress wowed as she stopped by the carpet to get photographed before going to celebrate the premiere of her new film Miller’s Girl at the festival. After pictures surfaced of her on X (formerly Twitter) fans couldn’t help but gush about how great she looked.

Jenna Ortega attends the Palm Springs International Film Festival

To the gala, the 21-year-old went all-out white. She wore a white flower-themed mini dress and high heels. The TV star also wore a white coat throughout the event to keep herself warm.

The celebrity wore her dark brunette locks down and a minimal makeup look. A white choker and silver dangling earrings finished off the Netflix star’s premiere ensemble. Jenna seemed to be in good spirits, as while she was on the carpet she was all smiles and at one point couldn’t stop laughing.

Miller’s Girl, also stars Hollywood actor Martin Freeman, Dagmara Dominczyk, Bashir Salahuddin, and Gideon Adlon. The thriller-comedy movie centers on a student, her teacher, and a creative writing assignment she is given. The flick is set to be released at the end of this month.

Jenna Ortega’s co-stars were also out to impress at the Palm Springs International Film Festival. Martin wore a gray suit and colorful loafers. The film’s director, Jade Halley Bartlett (who is making her directorial debut with the film) wore a long black gown, while Adlon sported a black mini dress and some matching heels.

Talking about the film previously, she said she was “really curious to see how that one turns out.”

She explained her character, Cairo Sweet, was the “most complex” one she had ever played. “The material is a bit risqué. I think it’s the kind of film that motivates conversations and kind of forces dialect on a subject that makes people very uncomfortable.”

She said doing films that make people “uncomfortable” is “interesting.”

Francesco Totti's departure from lifelong club Roma has taken a step closer after it was revealed the 39-year-old has been looking at houses in Madrid. 

The iconic Giallorossi captain is currently in the twilight of his career and pondering his future and is yet to be offered an extension on his contract.


And it looks like his future is unlikely to be at the Stadio Olimpico after a press release on Saturday from real estate and finance organisation Proto Group.

The consulting company, who work with the likes of David Beckham, Lionel Messi and Edinson Cavani, have announced they have been enlisted by Totti to help find him a Villa near Madrid.

'The player has already visited some homes in the most exclusive part of the city, but for the moment it has not yet been decided,' they said.

'This is an investment that the Totti family, who have just had have another child, would like to do.'

The Italian and his wife Ilary welcomed their third child, baby daughter Isabel, into the world on Monday.

News of his proposed move comes in the midst of reports linking the forward with a sensational move to Real Madrid to see out his career, a connection which Roma have adamantly denied.

Totti fuelled speculation after he told Mediaset 'the Santiago Bernabeu is an amazing stadium. Real Madrid are my only regret,' after he received a standing ovation from the Bernabeu crowd in Roma's Champions League defeat to the Spanish giants.

Proto Group did address the rumours stating his 'decision to take a house in Madrid is not necessarily related to the player's transfer to Real Madrid.'

Totti has been at Roma for the entirety of his career, scoring 300 goals in 751 appearances since his first-team debut in 1993.

Francesco Totti waves from the bench during the Serie A match between Udinese and AS Roma on Sunday

Totti revealed regrets over turning down Real Madrid move after standing ovation from Bernabeu crowd

He helped Roma to win a Serie A title in the 2000-01 season as well as the Copa Italia twice in 2007 and 2008. 

But his deal at the Stadio Olimpico is set to expire in June and despite still being a firm fan favourite, he has not featured prominently in manager Luciano Spalletti's plans.

He has seen his game-time significantly diminish this term. The former Italy international has made just eight appearances in all competitions.

Confronted in a press conference about Totti's future, Roma chief executive Mauro Baldissoni looked to dodge accountability.

He said: 'Totti is talking to president James Pallotta. They'll talk to each other, and then they'll talk some more. 'It's a delicate subject because it has to do with the final part of a legend's career. So it's right that the discussion should be between them.'

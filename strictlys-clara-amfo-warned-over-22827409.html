Strictly's Clara Amfo warned over sexy outfits and racy routines by her mother

July 27, 2024
blog

BBC Radio 1 presenter Clara Amfo has revealed that her mother has already warned her about wearing sexy outfits ahead of her Strictly Come Dancing debut.

The 36-year-old radio personality is set to show off her moves when the BBC dancing competition returns to our screens later this month.

As well as receiving helpful tips from her colleagues Scott Mills, Mollie King and Dev, who have all taken part in the show previously, Clara has let slip that her mother Gracie has been offering her some words of wisdom.

Speaking to Daily Star Online at a press conference about the new series, Clara said: "My sweet mum has already shared some opinions on how I should present myself when it comes to the outfits.

"She's like 'You know you don't have to wear anything to short,' and I'm like 'Mum, there will be some dances that require me not to wear many clothes,'

"She's like 'Just make sure it's sensible, okay'."

As well as sexy outfits, Clara admitted that she's not sure how her mother will feel seeing her getting up-close-and-personal with her dance partner during the more seductive dances.

"The Rumba is very sensual," the DJ noted.

"I've thought about this and if I get a chance to do the rumba, I know my mum's going to feel a way about it.

"The rational side of her will understand it is just a dance.

"The Rumba is different to like the Charleston or Samba. The Rumba is saying this is sex.

"When you watch two people dance the Rumba, you think 'are they?'"

Strictly super-fan Clara has revealed that joining the cast is not just about the dancing for her.

She explained: "Music is my everything so the song selection I'm obsessed with.

"Most of the reason why I'm doing this, as well as learning to dance, is because I just really want to be near Dave Arch and his band.

"I'm obsessed with musical arrangements. I'm always thinking about what song I'd like to dance to and 'how it would be arranged."

She added: "I love to dance but loving to dance is one thing and learning choreography is a whole other thing, so as long I can enjoy the song I'm trying to dance to, that for me is my main thing.

Clara is one of 12 celebrities who have signed up to take part in the show and she already knows who she's got her eye on as her biggest competition.

"I definitely feel like Jamie [Laing] is going to be a man on a mission," she said.

"It's like Jamie's revenge and I'm rooting for him. How annoying to break your foot when you're about to do Strictly."

The Made In Chelsea hunk was originally part of the 2019 cast but had to pull out of the show after picking up an injury in rehearsals.

Clara will find out who her dance partner is on the Strictly 2020 launch show this Saturday.

Speaking about her ideal pairing, she said: "I want someone who is going to work me hard.

"I don't wanna be wrapped in cotton wool. I like Beyoncé's mentality and I want my partner to be like that."

Strictly judges Shirley Ballas, Craig Revel Horwood and Motsi Mabuse will be back to offer their expert advice, but Clara admitted that isn't too worried about receiving constructive criticism.

She said: "There is a messed-up part of me that is low-key looking forward to Craig Revel Horwood saying something shady to me. That makes the experience real.

"The thing is I have been known to clap-back, but I'm coming on here to be judged and to learn to be better. I can handle the shade."

Strictly Come Dancing returns to BBC One on Saturday October 17

Professional tennis players and fans will tell you that Rick Macci is arguably the best coach tennis has ever seen. His career as a coach spans more than 40 years. Within that period, he coached some of the best players in tennis, many of whom went on to dominate tennis tours and became world number one. But perhaps what made Rick the best in business is his ability to spot talent, nurture it, and bring out the best in a player.

Rick Macci is an American tennis coach and former player. He is best known for his work coaching top tennis players, including Andy Roddick and Maria Sharapova. Although Macci has trained many professional tennis players, he is synonymous with being the coach of Venus Williams and Serena Williams.

Macci is United States Professional Tennis Association Master Professional and a 7-time USPTA national coach of the year. Since 1985, he has been running the Rick Macci Tennis Academy, one of the most outstanding tennis academies in Boca Raton, Florida.

He then went to study secondary school at Greenville Senior High School. Macci graduated in 1973 and enrolled at Wright State University, a national public research university in Dayton, Ohio. However, Macci only studied for two years at the university and became a pro tennis player.

Rick played tennis while young and became the top under-18 tennis player in Ohio. But Macci did not play tennis at a young age alone. Instead, he also played basketball and received Hall of Fame induction honours for his accomplishments in the two sports in his hometown in Ohio.

Professional career

Rick started his professional career in the early 1970s. First, he played tennis at a professional level at Bob Schul’s Sports Complex in Troy, Ohio. Then, in the late 70s, Macci played tennis at the Sinnet Indoor Tennis Club in Vineland, New Jersey. In 1979, he achieved the first Ranked Adult player in the New Jersey tournament.

After playing tennis for several years, Rick founded a tennis academy in 1985 called Rick Macci International Tennis Academy. His academy was in Haines City, Florida, at the Grenelefe Golf & Tennis Resort. Initially, his academy only had two students, Jennifer Capriati and Tommy Ho. Jennifer Capriati and Tommy Ho were the initial success stories to come out of the academy.

In 1988, Ho became the youngest male player in the open era to play in the main draw of the US Open singles at the age of 15 years and two months. Jennifer Capriati also set several youngest-ever records at the start of her career. She made her professional debut in 1990 at 13 years and 11 months old.

In 1992, she won a gold medal at the Summer Olympics and became a world No. 1 tennis player in 2001. After establishing his academy in 1985, Macci moved the academy to Delray Beach. Then, in the early 1990s, Macci met Serena Williams and Venus Williams.

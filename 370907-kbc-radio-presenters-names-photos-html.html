Can you tell KBC radio presenters names just by listening to their voice or do you rank amongst the people that use radio call-in sessions to send greetings to relatives or loved ones? If you are an avid listener of Swahili station then some of your favourite radio hosts must be from KBC. In the early 1920s, the only radio station that Kenyans could listen to was Kenya Broadcasting Corporation (KBC), formerly known as the Voice of Kenya (VOK). During that time, a radio would be placed in a strategic place to make it easier to listen to news, music, entertainment, sports, or local programmes.

However, as more radio stations were formed, KBC had to up their game to remain relevant to the old generation and millennials. Employing educated radio presenters that could attract hundreds if not thousands of listeners from across the country is one of the strategies that has paid off for this station.

Despite the evolution of television, most Kenyans listen to presenters at KBC radio Taifa Sauti ya Mkenya (voice of the people) just to send greetings or request their favourite songs. However, there is more to KBC radio than listening to music or the latest news; you can call or text and share your views about a particular topic. This works to educate fellow Kenyans.

If local programmes are what motivate you to switch on the radio then some of the shows presented by the most popular presenters at KBC include Tafrija, Zinga, and Taifa Freestyle Sunday edition. Club nation Saturday edition, Reggae Vibes, Ipikali, Mashariki East, Shida za Dunia are other entertaining shows.

Ever since it started, KBC has been home to some of the best journalists in Kenya. It is known to have produced some of the most talented male and female legends such as Omuga Kabisae, Jacqueline Kamonya, Khadija Ali Mohamed, Jeff Mwangemi, and Leonard Mambombotela.

They have played a significant role in shaping the media industry. KBC radio also has upcoming presenters that are making a name for themselves in this industry.

Thanks to their unique presenting skills, they leave listeners glued to the station. With a talented pool of presenters, listeners can get updates on what is going on not just in politics but also in sports and entertainment. Here are the popular radio presenters that call KBC radio their workplace.

Rebeccah Cherotich

Rebecca often called by her fans as Becky Cherotich is a female radio presenter in Kenya who has over eight years' experience in radio broadcasting. Her journalism career started in Biblia Husema, where her roles included voicing commercials, news anchoring, and presenting morning shows. She started working for KBC radio Taifa in 2012.

Since then, Cherotich has been a presenter for various shows, including Taifa Freestyle Sunday, Club Nation Saturday edition, Serebuka, and many others. Her eloquence for Swahili language and impressive communication skills has enabled her to win the audience's heart.

Edmond Selasia

Edmond hosts the popular KBC radio show Tafrija with Caroline Muramba. He also delivers great Rhumba music in the show Raba Raba where he talks to fans and takes their music requests. He studied journalism at Kenya Institute of Mass Communication (KIMC), and in 2008 he got a job working as a presenter at KBC. Pepa Afrika is another show Edmond has been known to host.

Caroline Mramba

If you are an avid fan of local radio stations, then you Know KBC Sauti ya Kenya presenter Caroline Mramba. She is a host for the show Tafrija and one of the female presenters in Kenya who loves to keep her personal life private.

However, she went to Molo secondary and studied journalism at Daystar University. By following her social media handles, you are likely to see more KBC presenters photos of your favourite hosts as she often posts them.

Kazungu Mwinyi

Kazungu is another talented radio presenter at KBC who hosts Top Mashariki. He interviews and presents musicians from all over Kenya and informs fans of what is currently trending. He attended high school at Mazeras High and studied Mass communication at Nairobi Aviation College.

The list of KBC presenter's names would not be complete without mentioning Bonnie Musambi. He has been the host for the show Zinga la Asubuhi since 2008. Momanyi Mathias

Fans of KBC radio know Momanyi as Double M. He studied journalism at Moi university and went to work for KBC radio as a presenter for various shows like Vuvuzela, Ramani ya Kiswahili and Kaulimbiu. Apart from informing listeners on multiple issues, Momanyi is also a producer at KBC.

Joyce Kahiga

Joyce is a KCB presenter famous for the show The Rock of Blessing, which airs on Sundays. Apart from being a diploma holder, she is also a Moi alumna. The multi-talented presenter also hosts the Top East Defender with Mwinyi Kazungu.

Joyce is also famous for hosting the show Pambo la Radio Taifa with Mathias Momanyi, which was renamed Vuvuzela. Apart from being a great radio host, she is also a mother and wife.

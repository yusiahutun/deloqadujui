Filming began on the Miranda July directed film in May.  This is July's first feature film since 2011's The Future. 

Her latest tale centers on a woman, played by Evan Rachael Wood, who has her world turned upside down when her criminal parents invite an outsider to join their biggest heist yet.

Gina's not just commanding attention on the set, she's also making a splash for her generosity.

Earlier this week, she announced she will use her "For Your Consideration" Emmy campaign funds from CBS TV Studios on a college scholarship that will pay for an undocumented high school Latina to attend Princeton University.

TV networks and studios shell out the FYC funds every year in advance of the Emmy nominations to help promote their submitted creatives and shows.

'Our show has always jumped at any opportunity to help me do something for the Latina community,' Rodriguez told Hollywood Reporter.

'So I asked my showrunner, Jennie [Snyder Urman], if we could do something different with the money this year.'

The actress told THR she wasn’t sure if she should go public with her good deed, but added that she’s hoping the news of it will inspire others to help where they can, too.

'It’s taboo to talk about the money being spent, but it’s the reality,' she said. 'I think sharing this might inspire other people to do something similar. You can desire recognition and, at the same time, decide to not play in the confines of the game as it’s set up.'

Giving back: The Jane the Virgin star is using her 2018 Emmy campaign money towards a college scholarship for an undocumented high school student; Gina pictured at the Emmys in 2017

The producer/actress, who is of Puerto Rican descent, has been active in philanthropic causes for years. 

She has served on the Hispanic Scholarship Fund’s board of directors since 2015, and worked with Big Brothers Big Sisters of Greater Los Angeles to find the perfect candidate for this scholarship award.

The unidentified recipient will begin attending Princeton University in the fall.

Gina first made her mark in Hollywood in the 2012 musical-drama film Filly Brown. She reached even bigger success in 2014 for her role as Jane Villanueva in the CW comedy-drama series Jane the Virgin.

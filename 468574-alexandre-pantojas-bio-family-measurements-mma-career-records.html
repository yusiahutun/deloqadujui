Career beginnings

Alexandre Pantoja started his fighting career by competing in local MMA fighting events in Brazil, specifically around Rio de Janeiro. His debut fight was a VF: Vila Fight event on July 21, 2007, in Rio de Janeiro against Antonio Carlos, where he won by submission.

On August 2, 2008, Pantoja competed in his second event - RF: Rocinha – against Peterson Malfort, where he won through knockout. He then experienced his first defeat during his third fight against William Vianna on September 25, 2008, during the Watch Out Combat Show 2 fighting event. He lost through a split decision.

Pantoja continued to compete in several MMA fighting events in Brazil for the next several years. These events include Shooto Brazil competitions, WFC: Pretorian Trials, MMA Fight Show, Watch Out Combat Show, and others. On December 9, 2014, Pantoja competed in his first international event, the Resurrection Fighting Alliance (RFA) Flyweight title match against reigning champion Matt Manzanares.

The fight was held in Albuquerque, New Mexico, USA, where he emerged the winner (and champion) through a rear-naked choke submission manoeuvre. On August 5, 2015, Pantoja once again competed in an RFA championship event against Damacio Page in Robinsonville, Mississippi, USA. Once again, he emerged victorious in the fight after downing his opponent through a submission.

Rise to fame

Pantoja’s increasing popularity and prowess on the international scene earned him a spot as one of the 16 contestants on the reality TV show, The Ultimate Fighter (TUF) in 2016. The contestants chosen were flyweight champions from various outfits worldwide.

In his first match, Pantoja faced off against Brandon Moreno (who would become the UFC flyweight champion later) to advance to the quarter-finals. In his second match, he competed against New Zealand MMA fighter, Kai Kara-France, winning by a unanimous decision. He then advanced to the semi-finals stage, where he, unfortunately, lost to the Japanese MMA fighter, Hiromasa Ougikubo.

Despite not being crowned the TUF champion, Pantoja’s performance attracted a lot of attention. As a result, he won a UFC contract, which saw him make his UFC debut in 2017 against Eric Shelton, which he won via split decision. He is still competing in the UFC flyweight division and is ranked #2 (as of August 1, 2022).

Gavin Whyte probed by police after being filmed masturbating in the street

March 12, 2024

Northern Ireland international footballer Gavin Whyte is facing a police probe after being caught on camera masturbating in the street during a drunken night out. 

The footage appears to show the Oxford United winger performing a sex act alongside a companion while the pair walk down a road singing.

The clip has received plenty of attention on social media, and the 22-year-old's club have now issued a statement confirming they will deal with the matter.

'OUTRAGING PUBLIC DECENCY'

Under the Criminal Justice Act of 2003, masturbating in the street could be considered to be an offence called Outraging Public Decency.

It is a common law offence that is triable in either the crown or magistrates' courts.

The maximum sentence if convicted in a magistrates' court a defendant could receive a prison sentence of up to six months, a fine of up to £5,000 or both.

For a conviction, a witness to the sexual activity would be needed.

'Oxford United will today meet with Gavin Whyte and warn him as to his future conduct after an incident back home in Northern Ireland at the weekend,' the statement reads.


'Gavin was filmed behaving in a way that is clearly unacceptable from a professional footballer and we will work with both Gavin and the Irish FA to ensure that he is aware of his responsibilities.'

When contacted by Sportsmail, a spokesperson for the Police Service of Northern Ireland said: 'Police are aware of a video on social media and are conducting enquiries into the matter.'

Northern Ireland manager Michael O'Neill, who gave Whyte his debut on September 11, 2018, has also hit out at the youngster.

'Gavin's actions in the video are clearly unacceptable and fall far below the standards that we expect from a Northern Ireland international.

Whyte (centre) is a Northern Ireland international and incident is said to be 'out of character'

The Police Service of Northern Ireland confirmed to Sportsmail that they are investigating

'I know that Oxford United will discipline him appropriately for this significant mistake and both his club and the Irish FA will put in place measures to make sure that he learns from it.'

Oxford manager Karl Robinson, whose side are currently embroiled in a relegation battle at the bottom of the third tier, also weighed in and believes the incident was 'totally out of character'.   

'Gavin is a quiet, respectful young man who is totally devastated by this,' Robinson said. 'I have rarely heard anyone so remorseful for something that he regrets and knows he shouldn't have done. 

'Clearly he had drunk too much, and we will deal with that too, but whether you are a footballer or not that sort of behaviour is wrong on every level.

'We will sit down with Gavin today and we will remind him in no uncertain terms of his responsibilities. We have psychological and behavioural support here to help him get through this. 

'He is a great kid and I hope the fans can forgive him for one moment of madness. '

Whyte joined the League One side from Irish Premiership high-flyers Crusaders in the summer, and it is understood the incident took place back home in Northern Ireland.

The 22-year-old recently made his international debut, too, scoring 108 seconds into a 3-0 win over Israel.

Not only are Oxford struggling on the pitch, but this unwelcome episode comes amid severe financial difficulties off it too.

The club have faced three winding-up orders in the last 10 months, with the latest overdue tax bill set to be settled in the next 24 hours.

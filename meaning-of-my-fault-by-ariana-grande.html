Meaning of my fault by Ariana Grande

May 30, 2024
blog

In Ariana Grande's song "my fault," the overall theme revolves around self-doubt, the pressure exerted by others, and the struggle to maintain one's sense of self. Through her lyrics, Grande expresses the internal turmoil she experiences when others project their insecurities onto her. While on the surface, the song may seem like a simple expression of frustration, a closer look reveals deeper and unexpected themes that contribute to the overall message.One standout lyric in the song is "A new day, another leaves, and I wonder why, I'm thinking 'Is it me?'" This lyric alludes to the constant cycle of people entering and exiting our lives. It explores the universal feeling of questioning oneself when faced with repeated patterns. The theme here is the doubt that arises within us when relationships end or people walk away. It highlights the tendency to internalize these departures and blame oneself, even when it may not be warranted.Another important lyric is "But you don't believe that I'm not how I seem." This line tackles the issue of perception and the pressure to fit into the molds that others create for us. The theme of authenticity arises here, as Grande expresses her frustration with others not seeing her true self. It delves into the idea that people often have preconceived notions of who we are, disregarding our actual complexities and layers. This expresses the need for acceptance and understanding, reminding us that it is vital to see others for who they truly are.Moving on to the pre-chorus, we have the lyric "For how long can I, long can I wait? I carry on, but you can't relate." This line encompasses the theme of emotional exhaustion and the feeling of being on different wavelengths with those around us. It delves into the frustration that comes with constantly waiting for understanding and empathy from others, who seem unable to comprehend our experiences. It highlights the need for genuine connections and mutual understanding in relationships, emphasizing the importance of feeling seen and heard.One striking lyric in the chorus is "But you make me feel I'm smaller when I'm not." This line touches on the theme of external forces influencing our perception of self-worth. It addresses the impact of others' judgment and how it can demean and erode our sense of self. This theme highlights the struggle to maintain confidence and resilience in the face of negativity or belittlement. It ultimately emphasizes the importance of self-validation and self-love in overcoming external pressures.Lastly, the lyric "But you walk around like you're some kinda god" brings forward the theme of power dynamics and the manipulation that can occur within relationships. It addresses the notion of someone else trying to exert control and dominance, leading to feelings of inferiority. This theme explores how the actions and behavior of others can have a profound impact on our mental state and overall well-being. It ultimately calls attention to the need for healthier boundaries and mutual respect in relationships.In conclusion, "my fault" by Ariana Grande tackles various themes that contribute to the song's overall message of self-doubt and the struggle to maintain one's sense of self. Through her thought-provoking lyrics, Grande delves into topics such as questioning oneself, the pressure to conform, emotional exhaustion, external influences on self-worth, and power dynamics. These unexpected and imaginative themes intertwine to create a relatable narrative, reminding listeners of the importance of self-acceptance, authenticity, and healthy relationships.

Bryce Harper came under fire after staring down rival Orlando Arcia and making a throat-slashing gesture as he led the Phillies to a 10-2 victory over the Braves.

Philadelphia now leads the National League Division Series 2-1 after Harper hit two homers in the win on Wednesday night.

Both times, as he made his way past second base, Harper stared at Arcia after the Braves star mocked him following game two of the series. Arcia was heard repeatedly joking: 'Ha-ha, atta-boy, Harper!' 


'I stared right at him,' Harper said. 'He can look wherever he wants,” Arcia responded. 'I can’t control where he looks.'

Then, after his second homer stretched the Phillies' lead to 7-1, Harper made a throat cutting gesture as he approached home plate.

Simpson then tried to play Arcia's role in the rivalry. 'There was a story attributed to some writer who said he overheard Arcia say something in the locker room that was disparaging about Harper after game two,' he said.

'There's no proof that it was even said - even the writer said he wasn't sure that's what he said - But Harper's taken it to heart and he's been glaring at Arcia as he rounds second on both homers.'

America Ferrera - who has a 29inch waistline - says it's 'crazy' she was labeled 'curvy' by Hollywoo

September 26, 2024
blog

America Ferrera is one of Elle magazine's cover stars for its Women in Hollywood issue.

In the interview, the 39-year-old longtime actress spoke about the body standards Hollywood imposes on women.

Getting candid about her experience in the entertainment industry in her early career, she noted, '...The idea that people were looking at me and saying, "That’s curvy" is crazy.'

She reportedly has a 29-inch waistline. 

While working on films like Real Women Have Curves and The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants, she considered her figure average, but she was lauded for breaking the mold.

'What’s so insane is, you go back and look, and I had a very average-size body,' she explained to writer Kayla Webley Adler.

Getting candid about her experience in the entertainment industry in her early career, she noted, '...The idea that people were looking at me and saying, "That’s curvy" is crazy'

America, who made her film debut at age 17, continued, 'Not that I care, but it’s like, that’s insane that we thought that was so groundbreaking. 

'I was Hollywood’s version of imperfect, which seems so ridiculous.'

She added that the experience was not unique to her: 'I don’t feel alone in that either. There are so many women who were called brave, just because they are people in bodies.'

The Honduran beauty is hopeful for the future of Hollywood, however.

'What I continue to wish for my career, and women’s careers and people of color’s careers, is that we don’t have to exist inside of these boxes or these lanes — that we don’t have to be relegated to represent just the thing that the culture wants us to represent,' she said.

Ferrera declared, 'I want to be more of who I am as a person, and to get to make art that doesn’t fit into any of the boxes and isn’t about the dominant conversation people have wanted to have about me because I’m a woman who doesn’t fit into stereotypical Hollywood.'

She's had a busy year as she starred in the summer box office smash Barbie alongside Margot Robbie and Ryan Gosling.

And next, she'll venture behind the camera as she directs the film I Am Not Your Perfect Mexican Daughter, based on a book by Erika L. Sánchez.

In October, America was featured as one of Glamour magazine's Women of the Year.

In the accompanying interview, she spoke on the theme of inequities and forging a career despite not having humble beginnings.

'I had always believed in myself because my mother had raised me to believe that, in the United States of America, being a poor, short, brown, fat daughter of immigrants did not preclude my dreams,' she said.

The Emmy-winning actress added, 'If anything, it made me an underdog, and there’s not much that the USA loves more than a good underdog story. I was determined to build a career in an industry that didn’t reflect people like me. I refused to be deterred.'

Incubus Performs Drive On The Late Show With Stephen Colbert

February 18, 2024
blog

As the ‘90s alt-rock icons Incubus prepare for their upcoming North American tour, the band appeared as the musical guests on Wednesday’s  The Late Show with Stephen Colbert, performing their fan-favorite hit “Drive.”

Drive, which comes from Incubus’ breakthrough 1999 album “Make Yourself” which also generated hits with “Pardon Me”, “Stellar” which all cracked the top 3 of the Billboard Alternative chart.

To mark the 20th anniversary of the release of “Make Yourself” Incubus recently announced plans to re-release the album along with additional material.

Incubus will support the album re-release with a major North American tour, which kicks off on September 13th, with a performance at the Fillmore in Denver. The tour will feature support from Dub Trio, Wild Belle, and Le Butcherettes.

“We are so looking forward to this fall tour,” Incubus’s  Brandon Boyd in a statement announcing the run. “Make Yourself was such a pivotal moment in our band and we are beyond excited to reintroduce this album to our audience while also showcasing the many musical roads it ultimately led us down. See you soon!”
